About the Center

About Us

The Cybersecurity Innovation Center at West Virginia State University advances cybersecurity education, research, and real-world innovation through hands-on labs, collaborative projects, and student-centered learning.

Its work emphasizes critical infrastructure protection, especially in agriculture and other essential sectors, while helping position WVSU as a leader in cybersecurity among HBCUs.

CyberHive

CyberHive is the center's IoT embedded systems lab, where students explore connected devices, hardware-aware security testing, and defensive design for smart environments.

Lab Profile

KITE Lab

KITE Lab supports applied security experiments around critical infrastructure technologies, resilience testing, and practical prototyping for emerging cyber-physical systems.

Lab Profile

Robotics and Autonomous Systems

This lab turns cybersecurity into a physical systems practice space through robotics, autonomous platforms, automation, and hands-on cyber-physical experimentation.

Lab Profile

Cybercity

Cybercity is a critical infrastructure penetration testing and defense simulation environment built for realistic training, attack emulation, and resilience-focused exercises.

Lab Profile

Honeynet

Honeynet is a deliberately vulnerable website designed for testing. It gives students a controlled environment for exploring web attacks, validating detections, and practicing defensive analysis without affecting production systems.

Research Team Profile

Rohith Reddy Battula

Research FocusMy research focuses on intrusion detection for Industrial Control Systems (ICS), with a specific interest in detecting Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs), a class of attacks that are notoriously difficult to detect because they are slow-moving, targeted, and designed to look like normal activity.
ApproachMost of my work has gone into developing hybrid IDS models that combine neural network architectures like CNN and Bi-LSTM to detect these threats more reliably than traditional approaches. I test these models on established benchmarks and use CUDA and HPC resources to handle the computational load of training deep learning models on large network traffic datasets.
Current WorkApplied mathematics, particularly statistical methods and self-similarity analysis, also plays a role in how I frame and validate detection problems. I am currently working under the supervision of Dr. Ali Al Sinayyid at West Virginia State University, where my most recent work has been accepted for publication in Scientific Reports (Springer Nature).

Research Team Profile

Sheri Vishnu Vardhan Reddy

Research FocusMy research focuses on AI-driven cybersecurity for Industrial Control Systems (ICS). I aim to solve the challenge of accurate and reliable cyberattack detection in critical infrastructure environments while minimizing false alarm rates.
ApproachMy approach involves leveraging deep learning techniques such as LSTM autoencoders, hybrid detection frameworks, and time-series analysis to analyze, optimize, and improve system performance and security outcomes.

Research Team Profile

Esther Dhiramo

Research FocusMy research focuses on intelligent intrusion detection for SCADA and Industrial Control Systems (ICS). I tackle the challenge of accurately detecting cyber intrusions and anomalies in critical infrastructure while minimizing false alarms and improving detection reliability.
ApproachMy work involves evaluating machine learning, deep learning, and hybrid intrusion detection methods across multiple datasets and performance metrics to optimize detection accuracy and strengthen overall system resilience.

Research Team Profile

Luice Khamboo

Research FocusMy research focuses on developing a machine learning-based system to detect phishing attacks that target financial services. After reviewing several research papers on cyberattacks across critical sectors, I identified phishing as one of the most common threats affecting financial institutions.
ApproachThe study uses phishing URL datasets to train and test machine learning models, with particular focus on Random Forest and Decision Tree models that have shown strong performance in prior studies.
GoalThe goal of this research is to design and evaluate an online detection model that can analyze incoming URLs and classify them as phishing or legitimate while generating alerts when a phishing attempt is detected.

Research Team Profile

Vaishnavi Kottala

Research FocusMy research focuses on DDoS for 5G networks. I aim to solve the challenge of accurately detecting and mitigating DDoS attacks in high-volume, low-latency environments.
ApproachMy approach combines machine learning and deep learning algorithms such as Random Forest, Decision Tree, SVM, Naive Bayes, CNN, RNN, LSTM, and XGBoost to analyze network traffic patterns, improve performance, and enhance real-time threat detection.

CyberHealth Basics

Cyber Bandaid

Cyber Bandaid is a beginner-friendly set of quick fixes and smart habits for everyday digital safety. It focuses on the simple actions that prevent avoidable problems before they grow.

Pick a Better Password

Use longer passphrases, avoid repeats, and mix unrelated words so your passwords are harder to guess and easier to remember.

  • Aim for length before complexity
  • Do not reuse passwords across sites
  • Use a password manager when possible
Try this challenging password game

Latest Fraud to Watch For

Be cautious with fake delivery texts, impersonation calls, urgent payment requests, and account-recovery scams designed to rush your decisions.

  • Pause before acting on urgent messages
  • Verify requests through known contact channels
  • Never send codes or money under pressure
Reference link to fraud watch

How to Spot Phishing

Look for mismatched senders, pressure tactics, suspicious links, and messages asking you to verify credentials or payment information.

  • Hover over links before clicking
  • Check for spelling and sender mismatches
  • Treat login-reset messages with caution
